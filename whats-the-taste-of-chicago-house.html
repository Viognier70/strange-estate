Music & Wine

Experience the taste of Old School Chicago House?

Strange Estate  ·  March 2026

Featured Wine

Underground White No. 3

A textured, aromatic white with generous fruit and moderate acidity — built for flow, not complexity.

Learn More Systembolaget

This style works because its structure and sensory profile mirror the atmosphere of old school Chicago house. The music is built on warm basslines, looping rhythms, and a steady, immersive groove rather than sharp contrasts or aggressive intensity. A textured, aromatic white wine with generous fruit and moderate acidity follows the same logic.

01
Round Mouthfeel & Soft Texture

Corresponds to the music's warm analog sound — the wine's smooth body provides a stable, enveloping sensory base, just like Chicago house's continuous bassline.

02
Ripe Fruit & Floral Aromatics

Early house music carries emotional warmth and openness. Aromatic white wines evoke a similar feeling through layered, inviting scents rather than austere precision.

03
Moderate Acidity

Maintains rhythm and balance — keeping the wine lively without becoming sharp or dominating, much like hi-hats and percussion that sustain energy without interrupting the groove.

04
Flow Over Complexity

Neither the wine nor the music relies on abrupt changes. Both create a continuous, pleasurable experience that encourages immersion, sociability, and repetition.

Finally, this style works because both the wine and the music emphasize flow over complexity. Neither relies on abrupt changes or intellectual intensity. Instead, they create a continuous, pleasurable experience that encourages immersion, sociability, and repetition — qualities that define both a great groove and a great glass of wine.
